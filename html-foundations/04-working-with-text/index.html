I put today in quotes, as of the time of making this, it has technically rolled over to 08/04/2022.

The Start of my Day

My day today would likely be seen as uneventful by most. But as for how my days have been recently, I'd consider it at least slightly eventful.

The day started with helping my mom grocery shop, as her back has been bothering her recently. Unfortunately, I had taken some nighttime aspirin last night, so when I woke up I was incredibly out of it.

The grocery shopping itself was about as exciting as grocery shopping could be. We did pick up some rotisserie chickens from the store for dinner though, and they made for a nice, easy dinner.

The Unproductive Middle

Following that, I spent a bit of time just relaxing and doing a whole lot of nothing. My allergies have been horrible lately (part of why I took some aspiring last night). So as much as I wanted to work on some stuff (or even play some Xenoblade Chronicles 3), my eyes were so irritated I could barely see.

For a bit I just kind of sat about on the computer, browsing through reddit a little and talking on Discord, but I was having troubled staying focused on account of wanting to tear my eyeballs out. So, eventually I just decided to lay down and watch some YouTube and rest.

The Productive Ending

Unfortunately, resting didn't work since I was feeling restless. But fortunately, my allergy medication had FINALLY kicked in. So, I decided it was time to be productive, and got up and about and began working a bit more on The Odin Project.

I had initially created a Xubuntu VM for it, but I really hated the window manager used in Xubuntu. Since my computer is strong enough (see what I did there? I really emphasized that word), I figured "what the heck", and just made a VM of the full version of Ubuntu 22.04 instead.

The setup was pretty straightforward, but there were a few exceptions (albeit small ones). For one, the mouse scroll wheel was a bit "stiff" feeling. I solved this through the use of the handy little tool imwheel.

Second, I was having some issues with using multiple monitors. It seemed like my mouse pointer was always slightly offset on the X-axis from where it should have been. My first assumption was that this had something to do with using monitors of different resolutions (2560x1440 and 1920x1080). I did some searching around for the general issue, and the main posts I found were some old ones stating certain old versions of Virtual Box Guest Additions had issues. But, during the process of looking it up, I toggled back and forth between full screen and not full screen in VirtualBox a few times, and suddenly as if by magic, it started working.

With the environment set up (and I'm leaving out a few things, like setting up git, installing Google Chrome and VSCode, etc.), it was time to get to work. I had left off right at the start of the HTML portion of the course, and that's what I'm doing right now. Wow!.
